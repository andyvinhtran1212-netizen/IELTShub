Speaking Practice

Work through Parts 1, 2, and 3 just like the real IELTS exam.

Part 1 · 4–5 minutes

The examiner asks general questions about yourself and familiar topics. Answer naturally — aim for 2–3 sentences per answer.

Tips for Part 1

  • Don't give one-word answers — extend with a reason or example.
  • Use natural fillers: "Well, to be honest...", "That's a good question..."
  • Speak at a comfortable pace — don't rush.
Part 2 · 3–4 minutes

You get a cue card topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1–2 minutes.

Part 3 · 4–5 minutes

The examiner asks abstract discussion questions related to the Part 2 topic. Give detailed, reasoned answers.

Tips for Part 3

  • Use the PEEL structure: Point → Example → Explanation → Link.
  • Show both sides: "On one hand... On the other hand..."
  • Use high-band phrases: "It could be argued that...", "This raises the question of..."
